Software Developer

A software developer is someone who makes or alters code to be used in hardware




Why am I interested in this career

My dad is the main reason that I chose a career in software development. When I was growing up I always saw him working on his computer coding for hours, which made me want to code for two reasons. The first being that he was my dad and so whatever he did seemed cool but the second reason is the reason that I'm still coding. The second reason is that I like how I could do anything I wanted with the code. I could make an informational website about a topic I find interesting, I could turn an idea into a game. I could do anything I wanted which interested me as a kid and still does.



Earnings

The average earning range for a software developer is about 100k to 250k.



Education

A job in software development and most computer science careers require at least a bachelor's degree. A bachelor's degree in software engineering is ideal, but studying computer science or other STEM programs such as math, science or engineering could help as well.



Working Conditions

Most software development jobs will have you sitting in an office at a desk. Some jobs may have you travelling to places to work with other people or going to conventions to show of your programs but most jobs will only include sitting at a desk for hours, coding.



Traits and Abilities

Some traits of a Software Developer are smarts, focus, adaptability, and learning skills.



Job Outlook

The job outlook for positions in software development is expected to grow by 25% which is more than usual for most jobs. As more technology is developed and spreads around the US and the world the need for people who can manage it and put it to use is growing at a very fast rate.



Benefits and Challenges

Some of the benifits of working as a software developer are that you can work from home if you need to or are on a trip. This can make it easier to spend time with your family and can let you take more vacations with them. Some of the dissadvantages of being a developer are that you have to work for long spans of time on one project which can make it boring after a while. Another problem you might face in this career is that you could have to learn new languages for different projects and you could also face other things you haven't seen before which could make it challenging.



Related Occupations

Some related occupations to being a software developer are Graphic Designer, IT Technician, Coding Tutor, and Technical Consultant.



10 Year Journey to Becoming a Software Developer

First year after high school: You applied to a college your senior year and got accepted so you decide to move out immediately to start college. You sign up for multiple tech related classes such as Computer Science, Software Engineering, Robotics, and a couple math classes just to be sure. Second year: You passed your classes and have learned so much more about coding. You sign up for more coding classes but on the side you start to do little coding gigs to make a little money. Third year: You keep on your way to a Bachelors degree in Software Engineering and Mathematics. You have made a couple hundred dollars from side gigs and send an application to a coding company like Google or Meta. Fourth year: This is your final year on your way to get a degree in Software Engineering. You got an interview at the company you applied to and it went well. They are considering you for a position as an amateur software developer in the company. Fifth year: You passed all of your classes and recieve a bachelors degree in Software Engineering! The company you interviewed with has hired you as an amateur software developer and have started working on a minor project with a team. Sixth year: You get promoted to a bigger role on a major project which has good pay. You salary is about 190k right now and you are living comfortably in a house by yourself. Years 7-36: You continue on your journey for another 29 years slowly climbing up the ranks and earning loads of money. Eventually you get married and have kids which thanks to your job as a developer you can spend time with your kids and wife. You decline multiple promotions to a project manager where you don't get to code as much. You would much rather code yourself than boss people on how to do it. After working at this company for nearly 38 years you decide to retire at 60. After 38 years of coding you have amassed about $150,000 in savings and can live the rest of your life well. Year 37: You keep coding in your free time because you love it so much and from time to time you will do gigs like you did when you were in college. Year 38: You eventually die at the age of 95. Your family lives the rest of their life comfortably because of your wealth.
